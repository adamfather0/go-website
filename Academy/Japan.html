The Nihon Kiin (Nippon Kiin) is Japan's premier go organization, governing and promoting the game of go. With its headquarters in Tokyo, the Nihon Kiin oversees a network of professional go players numbering around 300. To ensure comprehensive coverage of the country, the organization has established regional headquarters in Nagoya and Osaka, serving the Chubu and Kansai regions, respectively.

Founded in 1924 through the unification of Honinbo-mon and Hoensha, two influential go organizations of the time, the Nihon Kiin has played a pivotal role in shaping the landscape of Japanese go. Despite facing challenges from rival organizations, the Nihon Kiin has solidified its position as the dominant force in Japanese go. Nihon Kiin Title Matches

The seven major go tournaments in Japan, ranked by prize money, are Kisei, Meijin, Honinbo, Oza, Tengen, Gosei, and Judan. The top three tournaments, Kisei, Meijin, and Honinbo, with the highest prize money, are collectively known as the "Three Great Titles." Among them, Kisei is the highest title. If a player wins all three titles, they are said to have achieved the "Grand Slam."

There are many professional go tournaments, but what sets the "Seven Major Titles" apart? The biggest characteristic of the Seven Major Titles is that all professional players are eligible to participate, although the methods for determining challengers vary. In the Meijin and Honinbo tournaments, players must advance to the league stage after qualifying. Reaching the league stage is proof of being a top-level player.

  • Tengen: 20,000,000 yen
